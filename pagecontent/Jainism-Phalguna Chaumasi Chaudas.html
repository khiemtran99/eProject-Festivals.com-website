Phalguna Chaumasi Chaudas

Publish Date: Wed 20, March 2019

Remaining:

Chaumasi Chaudas is one of the most important festivals for Jains. Stemming from the teachings and philosophy of Lord Mahavir and the pathfinders of the religion, this festival is the manifestation of the principle of Ahimsa or non-violence and charity. There are several dimensions to this celebration that have changed from time to time but the objective of this festival is to provide an occasion to reflect on the teachings of Mahavir and practice them intensively for the good of oneself and the world at large.

The Chaturmas or Chaumas corresponds to the period of four months that starts from Ashadi Purnima (full moon during June-July) and ends on the Karthika Purnima (October - November). This four month period in India is marked by rains throughout and is therefore called as Varsha Vaas.

The rains bring about rich vegetation making the earth green all around. Jainism views plants too as lives with pains and feelings and therefore enjoins not hurting them too. Therefore in order to prevent the human movements hurting the plants during these four months, it was advised that people remain indoors

Especially the Jain Munis commit themselves to prayers and meditation during these four months seeking the welfare of all. Though this logic might not apply to the modern life where the roads are sophisticated and free of any vegetation, this concept of ahimsa marks the spirit behind the Chaumasi Chaudas.

The main purport of the event is to enjoin people and the practitioners of Jainism to involve in an austere and meditative life during these four months. We can call these four months as an intensive phase or rigorous period of practising the essential teachings of Jainism including ahimsa. The Jains clean their homes and shops are prepare for the best part of their lives.

Since during the monsoon time the humans are vulnerable to a variety of infective diseases, restricting movements and food habits and resorting to a healthy way of life practically helps avid several troubles and promotes good health and happiness.

The whole of life takes a new turn over these four months. Taking holy baths in rivers and observing fasting are done predominantly during this period. Jain Munis completely commit themselves to meditation and penance. Though this does not apply to the common people these days, the Jain Munis confine themselves to their ashrams and do not travel.

Jains are enjoined to take only a single meal during these days. The Jain merchants prayerfully get the Jain Munis to their homes to guide them in contemplation on the higher truths of life and listen to their good teachings.

Yet another importance of Chaumasi Chaudas ensues from the fact that an important event for Jains namely the Paryushana falls during this period. This festival concludes with the event named Kshamavani Diwas. This is the day when the Jains seek forgiveness from each other for any hurts intended or unintended. Thus, Chaumasi Chaudas highlights the values of ahimsa, charity, good deeds, spiritual living and forgiveness.
